Community Wiki
When did you start that?

With so many new community-led projects starting every day its hard to keep up. I wanted to give a quick round up of all the cool stuff we've got going on, in case well you know, you missed it.


  • Meetups - Live events happen every Wednesday at 5pm UK/9am PST. Join the zoom call here lead on this @Demetrios
  • Coffee sessions - tend to get released once per week (recommend subscribing to youtube or spotify to stay up to date) lead on this @Vishnu Rachakonda
  • Reading club - Happens every 2 weeks. More info here and stay up to date in #reading-group lead on this @Charlie You
  • Engineering Labs - A new cohort is launched every 5 months. We just finished the most recent one. stay up to date in #engineeringlabs lead on this @IlNardo92
  • Office Hours - We meet every Thursday at 4:30 UK/ 8:30am PST. No recording, no fluff. Just people talking about what they are working on and learnings they can share. stay up to date #officehours lead on this @Harry Berg
  • MLOps Stacks - Open source project to help make plugging together tools easier. Follow or contribute in #mlops-stacks lead on this @Luke Marsden
  • Women of MLOps - Feminine energy united! Stay up to date in #women-of-mlops lead on the @Aparna Dhinakaran
  • Tooling Working Group - A working group that is helping create the frameworks for tools in each part of the ML Dev lifecycle. Lead @Demetrios
  • AIIA - The AI Infrastructure Alliance. An alliance of ML tooling companies with the goal of establishing a canonical stack for MLOps. Lead @Dan Jeffries

Apart from this, we have started to create channels for different tools and oss projects. If you do not see a channel for a tool you want to know more about or talk about, let me know and I will make one!

ML flow + AWS
Deploy MLflow in an AWS app runner

Community member Douglas Trajano wrote a nice blog tutorial on that very topic! Check out some excerpts below.

'AWS launched in May 2021 the AWS App Runner, a fully managed service that makes it easy for developers to quickly deploy containerized web applications and APIs.

Unlike AWS Fargate, AWS App Runner has a pricing policy based on requests that your application receives. Apps that don’t receive requests all the time can benefit from it.

Our loved MLflow fits perfectly in this context. The interaction with MLflow is usually made in training jobs, data scientists evaluating their experiments, or APIs that expose our models. In all these cases we don’t have frequent access, it occurs eventually.'
